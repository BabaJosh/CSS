What is Treaty 1?

Treaty 1 was the first agreement between Indigenous peoples and the Canadian government in Manitoba, signed in August 1871. It involved the Anishinaabe (Ojibwe) and Muskegon Cree nations. This treaty allowed settlers to use the land while promising benefits to Indigenous communities. (Source: Government of Canada)

Key Promises

  • Each family of five received 160 acres of land.
  • Annual payments of $3 per person.
  • Schools and farming tools were promised.

Treaty 1 is unique because it set the pattern for later numbered treaties. Unlike others, it included "outside promises" (additional verbal agreements) that were later written into Treaty 2. (Source: Treaty Relations Commission)

Treaty 1 Today

Communities like Long Plain First Nation use treaty rights for economic projects, such as gas stations and urban reserves. This helps create jobs and sustain their culture. (Source: Treaty 1 Nation)
